HOW TO SPOT A PHISHING EMAIL
Do not click on any malicious link.
Do not open any surprising attachment.
Read your emails cautiously and report anything that seems suspicious.
Never give your personal information.
It is common for phishing emails to instill panic in the recipient. Do not panic and read the emails patiently.
THINK BEFORE CLICKING
Don’t assume an email , text or phone call is authentic. Confirm contact details first.
Do not panic or rush. A genuine organization won’t mind waiting for your response.

DO YOU CHECK URLS?

If you receive an email from an organization that includes an HTML link in it, click check the link and you will see the full URL appear. If the URL does not include the organization’s exact name,
or if it looks, delete it because it’s probably a phishing email.
